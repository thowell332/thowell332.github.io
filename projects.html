Automated Mast Sorting Device

August 2018 - May 2019

At the behest of the Duke Forest, I worked in a team of 5 students to design and prototype an automated sorting device for tree nuts, known as mast, to operate via purely mechanical sorting methods. I later revisited the project in a new team of 3 students to build a higher fidelity prototype by machining aluminum and polycarbonate components to replace the original, primarily wooden device. We also redesigned the device to make it smaller, more compact, and easier to transport.

Poster

After the completion of the first prototype, we presented our device at the Duke First-Year Design Showcase. We created this poster to accompany our presentation at the poster session.

Model

Before building the second prototype, we created a 3D model of the revised design. In order to make the device smaller, we implemented a two-part system consisting of an upper collector and a lower collector. The upper collector is built from a flexible net stretched over a PVC frame. This net is designed to filter out large debris which could potentially clog the smaller lower collector. The design of the lower collector is largely inherited from the first prototype, though in this iteration it is made much smaller and is constructed from higher fidelity materials. To compare this device to the original prototype, please see our poster which was created after the initial prototype was completed.

Documentation

We documented our project after the completion of the second prototype for submission to the Duke Forest with a written report. The report contains background information about mast and explains the motivation for this project. It also goes into more detail about the design decisions and functionality of the final device, including a Python simulation which was used to determine some device dimensions.

Nanocomposite Fabrication for FDM Printing

July 2017 - August 2017

In a team of three students and under the direction of Dr. Miriam Rafailovich, I studied thermal conductivity in FDM printing and fabricated polymer nanocomposites for improved mechanical strength and thermal properties in 3D printed parts. Polylactic acid and polypropylene were used as the base polymers for composite material fabrication with graphene nanoplatelets and boron nitride nanoparticles. This research was conducted at Stony Brook University as part of the Garcia Center for Polymers at Engineered Interfaces.

Documentation

To document our research, we wrote a 21-page paper which provides background information on the project, details our materials and methods, and explains the results we achieved.

Poster

In November of 2017, I was invited to present our research at the Materials Research Society Fall Meeting & Exhibit in Boston, MA. I created this poster to accompany my presentation at the poster session.

Captain America Shield Launcher

February 2019 - March 2019

As an exercise in practical engineering and manufacturing techniques, I worked in a team of 3 students to design an injection moldable McDonald's Happy Meal toy that we later presented to a team of professional engineers responsible for designing real Happy Meal toys. In preparation for this toy fair, we sent our design to an independent manufacturing company to be professionally 3D printed for a high quality prototype.

Documentation

To document our project we composed a technical memo detailing our ideation process, toy description, and marketing analysis. The memo presents information about the manufacturability of the toy, including relevant design choices and projected cost figures. All technical drawings and CAD renderings are also included in the documentation.

Model

We modeled the toy in seven parts: the shield, shield mount (not visible), launcher top half, launcher bottom half, wristband, front spring pad (not visible), and back spring pad (not visible). The rendering on the right was generated in SolidWorks using the complete toy assembly. An exploded view of the model as well as technical drawings for each of the seven parts can be found in our project documentation.

Marketing Sheet

To accompany our presentation at the toy fair, we created a marketing sheet which outlines the assembly, functionality, safety features, and manufacturability of our toy.
