Nanocomposite Fabrication for FDM Printing

July 2017 - August 2017

In a team of three students and under the direction of Dr. Miriam Rafailovich, I studied thermal conductivity in FDM printing and fabricated polymer nanocomposites for improved mechanical strength and thermal properties in 3D printed parts. Polylactic acid and polypropylene were used as the base polymers for composite material fabrication with graphene nanoplatelets and boron nitride nanoparticles. This research was conducted at Stony Brook University as part of the Garcia Center for Polymers at Engineered Interfaces.
